SURVEY REPORT
THIS IS TO CERTIFY
that at the request of Underwriters concerned, survey has been held on the steel built, twin
screw, motor driven, offshore support vessel: -
“BOURBON EVOLUTION 802”
GT 6146 of Luxemburg
while at Carena Ship Repair, Abidjan, for the purpose of ascertaining the cause, nature and
extent of damage alleged to have been sustained in the following circumstances:
10 JANUARY 2017: CRANKSHAFT DIESEL GENERATOR # 3 BREAKDOWN
It was reported during normal operation, two engines DG3 and 5 were working in parallel on
load due to cranes operation. DG3 lubricating oil filters differential pressure high alarm
occurred, and the engine was stopped by emergency stop.
Oil filters were inspected and metal particles were found inside the filters.
Crankshafts were inspected and found in good condition. Crankcase was inspected. All
connecting rods were inspected. Unit 4RB connecting rod close to connecting rod cap was
found discoloured. Unit 4RB and 4LB connecting rod caps were removed. Connecting rod
bearings were found damaged and crankpin No.4 found seized

The bearing caps and bearing shells on both bank were darkened by heat damage. The
bearing shells were deformed and surfaces wiped.
The connecting rods were also noted to have heat damage.
The crankpins were found to be severely darkened from overheating and severely
scratched.
Oil sample taken from the crankcase showed a large presence of metallic particles.
We reattended the vessel on 24 February 2018 and the following was noted:
The owners subsequently ordered and installed a new diesel generator engine to replace
the damaged DG#3. At the time of our second attendance, the installation was completed,
and the engine was being commissioned.
The damaged old engine had been taken out of the vessel to the Carena Ship Repair
yard engine workshop where it was sighted.
The engine was partially dismantled to enable inspection of the damage in the crankcase
to be undertaken.
The nos 4 & 5 bearings shells were noted to be severely scored and scratched with the
copper underlay exposed in the case of no. 5 unit.
Inspection of the corresponding position on the crankshaft found the crankpin severely
grooved and scored over approximately 80% of the surface area at unit 4.
The crankshaft was damaged beyond economic repair and would have to be replaced.
ALLEGATION
No formal written allegation of cause has been made in respect of this damage. It is
understood that if a claim is to be made, the Owners will notify their Brokers of the allegation
of cause. In view of the foregoing, the damage now found and reported upon are noted Without
Prejudice to Underwriters’ liability and the Owners Representative has been so advised.
Remarks
Even though there is no formal allegation, the following has been noted:
1) The Owners reported in their email dated 7 March 2018 from Douchras Jean-Patrick that:
Quote Makers has reported that engine block may have a defect (from factory or yard)
which generated this breakdown. Makers advised to not repair it because the same issues
may happen again end of Quote.
2) It is noted, as also indicated in the same email and our Advice No.1, that this is the second
time the failure has occurred around the same 10,000 running hours (maximum
recommended hours being 24,000 hrs before overhaul) within 2 years.
3) In view of the above, we have requested that the manufacturers provide further
information to confirm their position. If confirmed, we could possibly then conclude on
what is the most likely cause of the damage.

SURVEYORS NOTES
We were assisted by the technician from Mecatlantic Mr Philippe Raymond.
Records indicate that the DG3 had a similar breakdown in February 2015 with the same
no. 4 unit being damaged at the time.
Due to the restrictions in the engine room, it was agreed that in order for a full assessment
of the damage to be done, including examination of the main bearings, especially in way
of no. 4 crankshaft unit, it would be better for the DG3 to be taken out of the vessel to
shore workshop for disassembly.
Alignment of crankshaft and main bearings to also check and an examination of material
to be undertaken.
A review of the engine room maintenance records indicated that the maintenance
schedules were being adhered to. The DG 3 running hours since last overhaul was less
than half the recommended 24,000 hrs. The last lubricating oil change was 367 hrs since
last overhaul and crankshaft examined on 4.5.17.
The technician from Mecatlantic has indicated that it would be faster to provide a
replacement engine while the damaged one is carefully examined and repaired if
considered a viable option.
The manufacturers subsequently indicated (as per email of 7.3.2018 from owners) that
the engine had a manufacturing defect and advised that if it is repaired the defect could
occur again, the owners as a result, opted not to repair the damaged engine.
The manufacturers subsequently indicated that the engine had a manufacturing defect,
the owners as a result opted not to repair the damaged engine.
